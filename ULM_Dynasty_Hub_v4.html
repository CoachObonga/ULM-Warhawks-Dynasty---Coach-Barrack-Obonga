Lore & Story Archive

Narrative history · key moments · dynasty lore

The foundation (2025–2028 · Years 1–4)
First-time HC Barack Obonga arrives at ULM with a D+ prestige rating and a spread offense, inheriting a program that had never made a bowl game. Year 1 ends 6–7 with a heartbreaking triple-OT bowl loss on a failed 2-point conversion. Year 2 brings a 3–9 sophomore slump. The program nearly fires Obonga after Year 4 (5–8). Instead, both coordinators are replaced heading into Year 5.
Breaking through (2029–2031 · Years 5–7)
New coordinators James Anderson and Alex Carman transform the program. Year 5 ULM breaks a 9-year Louisiana Tech losing streak en route to a 7–6 bowl season — the program's first bowl win over FIU, 52–50 in the Boca Raton Bowl. Year 7 (2031) brings a school-record 12–2 finish, a #25 national ranking, Doug Eby as a Heisman finalist, and ULM's first Sun Belt West Division title.
The Heisman era (2032–2036 · Years 8–12)
2032: Barry Abreu wins the first Heisman in program history. ULM goes 11–3, perfect 9–0 in the Sun Belt, and makes its first CFP appearance. Obonga declines the Michigan State HC job to stay in Monroe. 2034: James Laird wins the Heisman. ULM joins the Big 12 and wins the conference championship in Year 1, reaching the CFP Semifinal. 2036: Tyler Portilla wins the third Heisman in five years. Three Heismans — all running backs. It defined who ULM was.
The 2037 national championship (Year 13)
ULM enters the season ranked #7 but loses twice in the regular season. As the #12 CFP seed, the Warhawks shock #5 Texas, demolish #4 Penn State 52–6 in the Sugar Bowl, and defeat #8 Georgia in the Peach Bowl. In the National Championship in New Orleans, Lawrence Crook's walk-off 26-yard field goal with 0:07 remaining — set up by Jelani Roland's 81-yard pick-six off pressure from Neil Schleuger — seals a 17–15 title over #2 USC. ULM's miracle run is complete. The program that couldn't win a bowl game in 2025 was national champion in 2037.
The Colorado rivalry
They became ULM's defining nemesis the moment the Warhawks joined the Big 12. Colorado and ULM have met eight times, splitting the series 4–4 — but the location tells the real story. ULM is 3–0 at home, 1–4 anywhere else. The lateral interception with 24 seconds left in 2037. The two-kickoff-return-touchdown CCG collapse in 2035. Gammon's 353-yard evisceration in the 2038 championship game. Colorado has broken ULM's heart on the biggest stages. The rivalry isn't over.
The coordinator factory
ULM's greatest program strength is its ability to produce coordinators who leave for Power 4 HC jobs, replaced seamlessly from within. Anderson left for Mississippi State HC. Carman left for Wisconsin DC. Ramsey and Washington both departed after 2036. Each time, Tank Williams and Bulldog Henderson — 13 and 12 years on staff respectively — stepped up and preserved the dynasty's identity without missing a beat.
The 2038 exodus
Nine players drafted in a single year. Six in rounds 1–3. The 2038 NFL Draft gutted the roster in ways that couldn't be patched overnight. A 0–3 Big 12 start followed. A CCG loss to Colorado. An Alamo Bowl loss to Boise State on a last-second score. The dynasty didn't end — but it entered a new phase. The question heading into 2039 is whether Obonga can reload the way he once rebuilt.

Step-by-step instructions for getting this hub into Notion with tabs

Step 1 — Download the markdown export
Go back to the Claude chat and download the file ULM_Warhawks_Dynasty_Notion_Export.md. This is the full content in a format Notion can import directly.
Step 2 — Import into Notion
1. Open Notion → click + New page in the sidebar
2. Title it: ULM Warhawks Dynasty Hub
3. Click the ··· menu (top right of the page) → Import → Markdown & CSV
4. Select your downloaded .md file
5. Notion imports all headers, tables, and content automatically — takes ~10 seconds
Step 3 — Build the tab structure (sidebar navigation)
Notion doesn't have a native sidebar-tab system like this hub, but you can recreate it two ways:

Option A — Sub-pages (recommended):
Each section (Overview, Schedule, Roster, etc.) becomes its own sub-page. On your main Hub page, type /page and create a page for each tab. Then copy-paste the relevant section into that page. Notion shows sub-pages in the left sidebar automatically.

Option B — Table of Contents block:
Keep everything on one long page. Add a /table of contents block at the top — it auto-generates clickable links to every heading on the page. Click any header and it jumps there instantly.

Option C — Linked Database view:
For the Schedule and Roster tabs, use a Notion database (table view). Each row = one game or one player. You can filter and sort. Type /table to start.
Step 4 — Format the content in Notion
After import, clean up the formatting:

• Big section headers (OVERVIEW, AWARDS, etc.) → highlight and press H1
• Sub-section headers → highlight and press H2
• Callout blocks (⚠️ notes) → type /callout and paste the text
• Stat card rows (131–64, home/away) → use /columns to put them side by side
• Any table → type /table or paste directly (Notion auto-detects markdown tables)
• Win/Loss badges → add colored text manually using the text color tool (A icon)
Step 5 — Add icons & cover
• Click Add icon at the top of your Hub page → use 🏈 or the ULM logo
